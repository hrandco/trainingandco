HR Essentials

The goal of human resources management is to hire, retain, and support the skilled, motivated, and diverse workforce that an organisation needs to succeed. The HR function is invaluable to any organisation because it removes obstacles or distractions that may deter employees from doing their best work. HR professionals create new policies and systems that motivate or reward employees.

Leading Others

As a manager or leader, if you want people to give you bigger projects, more staff, or more responsibility, or even if you want your staff to respect you as a team leader, you’ll need to invest time and effort to learn to delegate tasks. It's hard to be an effective leader when you are distracted by too many other tasks you shouldn't be doing. This training is suitable for any employee with people leadership responsibilities.

Compliance

Alcohol and drugs cost Australian workplaces an estimated $6 billion per year in lost productivity. The use of alcohol and other drugs can impact workplaces in many ways, affecting relationships, safety and productivity. All workplaces should promote a safe culture, and workers are expected to recognise and practise appropriate behaviours. This topic is suitable for all employees.

Work Health and Safety

Poor health and safety practices lead to personal harm and injury, but also because workers, managers, supervisors and employers have duties under the model WHS laws. Hazards and risks with varying potential consequences can arise for all workplace employees. This topic suits all employees covered by the model WHS laws, which include employees in Queensland, New South Wales, ACT, Tasmania, Northern Territory, South Australia and Western Australia.

What kinds of services do you provide?

HR and Co. Consulting offers comprehensive advice and support on all aspects of employee management for your business. Our services include:


  • Employment Relations: Workforce planning, employment contracts, Award interpretation, employee classifications and pay rates, recruitment, onboarding, induction, performance management, and change management.
  • Workplace Health and Safety (WHS): Policies and procedures, WHS audits, site inspections, and Worker's Compensation claim coordination and support.
  • HR Systems and Audits: HR audits, policies and procedures, employee handbooks, remuneration checks, and implementing Human Resource Information Systems (HRIS) tailored to your business needs.
